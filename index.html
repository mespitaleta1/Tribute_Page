GABRIEL GARCIA MARQUEZ

Nobel Prize in Literature

"Gabriel García Márquez was born in Aracataca (Colombia) on March 6, 1927.
He attended secondary school in San José from 1940 and finished his
baccalaureate at the Colegio Liceo de Zipaquirá, on December 12, 1946.
He enrolled in the Law School of the National University of Bogotá, but
abandoned it to devote himself to journalism and literature He married
in Barranquilla in 1958 with Mercedes Barcha, the daughter of an apothecary.
In 1959 they had their first child, Rodrigo and three years later, his second
son, Gonzalo, was born.In his last years he suffered from lymphatic cancer,
which caused his death on April 17, 2014, in Mexico City. His memoirs were
published in 2002 with the title of Living to tell."

Awards Received

  • 1955 - First Prize in the contest of the Association of Writers
    and Artists, for his story One day after Saturday.
  • 1961 - ESSO Colombian Novel Award for La mala hora.
  • 1971 - Honorary Doctorate from Columbia University in New York.
  • 1972 - Romulo Gallegos Award for One Hundred Years of Solitude.
  • 1981 - Medal of the French Legion of Honor in Paris.
  • 1982 - Aztec Eagle Decoration in Mexico.
  • 1982 - Nobel Prize for Literature.
  • 1985 - Forty years award from the Circle of Journalists of Bogotá.
  • 1993 - Honorary member of the Caro y Cuervo Institute in Bogotá.
